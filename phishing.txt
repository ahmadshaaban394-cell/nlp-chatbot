Phishing

Definition:
Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers trick people into revealing sensitive information or performing unsafe actions.

Phishing usually uses fake emails, fake websites, fake messages, or fake login pages that look legitimate.

How it works:
The attacker pretends to be a trusted person, company, bank, university, delivery service, or technical support team. The victim is pressured to click a link, download an attachment, enter a password, or share sensitive information.

Targets:
- Usernames
- Passwords
- Credit card information
- Bank account details
- One-time passwords
- Personal information
- Company credentials
- Student accounts
- Email accounts

Types:
- Email phishing
- Spear phishing
- Whaling
- Smishing
- Vishing
- Clone phishing
- Business email compromise

Spear phishing:
A targeted phishing attack aimed at a specific person or organization.

Whaling:
A phishing attack targeting high-level people such as managers, executives, or administrators.

Smishing:
Phishing through SMS or text messages.

Vishing:
Phishing through voice calls.

Impact:
- Account takeover
- Identity theft
- Financial loss
- Malware infection
- Data breach
- Unauthorized access to systems
- Reputation damage

Example:
A user receives an email pretending to be from a bank. The email asks the user to log in through a link. The link opens a fake website that steals the user's credentials.

Warning signs:
- Urgent language
- Suspicious links
- Unexpected attachments
- Sender address looks strange
- Grammar mistakes
- Requests for passwords
- Too-good-to-be-true offers
- Threats of account closure

Prevention:
- Verify email sources
- Avoid clicking suspicious links
- Check the website URL carefully
- Use multi-factor authentication
- Do not share passwords
- Report suspicious messages
- Use email filtering
- Train users about phishing signs
- Keep browsers and antivirus updated
- Open websites by typing the address manually

Related terms:
Social engineering, spear phishing, fake login page, credential theft, MFA, awareness training.
